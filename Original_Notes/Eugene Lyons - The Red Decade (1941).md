Summary:
Eugene Lyons' *The Red Decade* is a polemical history of the Stalinist penetration of American life from 1919 to 1941. The book argues that the Communist Party USA was never a legitimate domestic political movement but a "foreign conspiracy" and obedient "colonial agency" of the Kremlin. Lyons traces the party's evolution through the "Third Period" of revolutionary militancy into the "Popular Front" era, where Communists successfully masqueraded as "Twentieth-Century Americans" to infiltrate the New Deal, the CIO, and cultural institutions. The narrative exposes the "Cult of Russia-Worship" among American intellectuals who rationalized the Great Terror and the man-made famines, the exploitation of the "Innocents" in front organizations like the League for Peace and Democracy, and the eventual moral collapse of the movement following the Nazi-Soviet Pact and the party's subsequent campaign of sabotage against U.S. defense.

# 1. Origins and Early Tactics (1919–1929)
### **I. Introduction: The Taboo and the "Red-Baiter"**
*   **The Watershed Moment (June 22, 1941):** The book opens on the day Nazi Germany invaded Russia. This event instantly exposed the fraudulent nature of American Communist "principles."
    *   **The Somersault:** For 22 months (during the Hitler-Stalin Pact), American Communists were "isolationists" preaching "The Yanks Are Not Coming." Within hours of the invasion, they flipped to "interventionists," demanding immediate aid for Britain and the USSR.
    *   **Specific Examples:**
        *   The *American Peace Mobilization*, which had maintained a "peace vigil" picketing the White House to stop aid to Britain, instantly called off the picket and supported the war.
        *   The *American Youth Congress* and *National Maritime Union*, previously anti-war, immediately passed resolutions for unstinted aid to the Allies.
    *   **The Lesson:** The "crux of the matter" is that American Communist propaganda is determined *solely* by the Kremlin's needs. It has zero relation to American national interest. If the "line" coincides with American interests, it is a sheer accident.
*   **The "Red-Baiting" Shield:**
    *   Lyons identifies a specific "taboo" among American liberals: One may attack Hitler, Mussolini, or the Mikado without question, but attacking Stalin or his American agents is forbidden.
    *   **The Mechanism:** The cry of "Red-Baiter" acts as a silencer. It is a "conditioned reflex" designed to paralyze logic. It prevents discussion of man-made famines, purges, and forced labor by diverting the argument to "motives" or "private lives."
    *   **The Double Standard:** When the newspaper *PM* attacked General Krivitsky (a Soviet defector) after his death, liberals were silent. But when *PM* attacks a Stalinist, the cry of "Red-Baiting" goes up.
    *   **Lyons' Definition:** To break the taboo, one must accept the label. "If an attempt to tell the truth about Russia-at-home and Russia-abroad is red-baiting, then I proclaim myself a red-baiter."

### **II. The Five Ages of the Communist International (The Comintern)**
*   **The "International" Illusion:** The Comintern is "international" only in the sense that the "American Express Company is international"—it has branches in foreign countries, but the headquarters and control are strictly national (Russian).
*   **Dominance of the Russian Party:**
    *   At no point did the combined membership of all foreign parties equal the membership of the Russian party.
    *   Control of the purse strings (Moscow gold) guaranteed subservience. Foreign leaders (Browder, Pollitt, Thaelmann) were treated as "pensioners and poor relations" at the Lux Hotel in Moscow, waiting for handouts and orders.
*   **The Five Periods (The Zig-Zags of Policy):**
    *   **1. First Period (1919–1921):** *Immediate World Revolution.*
        *   **Context:** Russia besieged by foreign armies; belief that the Bolshevik regime could not survive without revolutions in the West.
        *   **Tactics:** Shrill, conspiratorial, outlawry. Appeals to bold romantics and social rebels.
    *   **2. Second Period (1921–1928):** *Agitation and "Boring from Within."*
        *   **Context:** The Russian Civil War ended; the NEP (New Economic Policy) began. Russia needed trade and stability, not immediate war.
        *   **Tactics:** "United Front." Infiltration of legal bodies (unions, parliaments). The "Trojan Horse" concept begins here.
    *   **3. Third Period (1928–1935):** *Ultra-Leftism and "Social Fascism."*
        *   **Context:** Stalin’s rise to absolute power and the First Five-Year Plan. Need for revolutionary slogans to cover domestic terror/famine.
        *   **The Theory:** Capitalism is collapsing immediately. The main enemy is not the Capitalist or the Fascist, but the "Social Fascist" (Socialists, Liberals, Labor Leaders) who delay the revolution.
        *   **Consequence:** This policy forced German Communists to fight Social Democrats rather than Nazis, directly enabling Hitler’s rise to power.
    *   **4. Fourth Period (1935–1939):** *The Popular Front (Democratic Facade).*
        *   **Context:** Fear of Hitler. Russia needed military alliances with France/England/USA.
        *   **Tactics:** "Communism is Twentieth-Century Americanism." Revolution is shelved. Communists pose as the ultimate democrats and patriots to woo liberals.
    *   **5. Fifth Period (1939–1941):** *The Nazi-Soviet Pact.*
        *   **Context:** Stalin allies with Hitler.
        *   **Tactics:** Return to isolationism. Britain and France are "imperialist warmongers." Sabotage of US defense industries.
*   **Key Insight:** The "line" changes not because American conditions change, but because *Russian* foreign policy requirements change.

### **III. The Birth of the American Party (1919–1921)**
*   **The "Alien" Nature:** Lyons argues radicalism itself is not alien to America (citing Roger Williams, Paine, John Brown, Debs, the I.W.W.). The Communist Party is "alien" because its *allegiance* is to a foreign state, not because of the nativity of its members.
*   **The 1919 Split:**
    *   **Demographics:** In 1919, the Socialist Party membership jumped to 108,000, but 53% were in "foreign-language federations" (Russian, Ukrainian, South Slavic). These recent immigrants were "Bolshevik to the core."
    *   **The Purge:** The Socialist Old Guard (Hillquit) expelled the Bolshevik wing to save the party.
    *   **The Triplets:** The expelled groups formed *three* separate Communist parties in Chicago (Sept 1919):
        1.  **The Communist Party:** Dominated by the foreign-language federations (the "Smolny" group).
        2.  **The Communist Labor Party:** Dominated by native English speakers (John Reed, Benjamin Gitlow).
        3.  **The Proletarian Party:** A Michigan splinter group.
*   **The Underground "Romance":**
    *   **The Palmer Raids:** Attorney General Palmer's raids drove the movement underground. This created a "titillating illusion" of fighting a desperate revolution.
    *   **Bridgeman, Michigan (1922):** A secret convention held in the woods by torchlight. It was raided by the police (who had agents inside). It revealed the amateurish, theatrical nature of the conspirators.
    *   **Membership:** Despite the noise, membership dropped from ~60,000 (pre-split) to barely 10,000 by 1923. 90% of the membership remained in non-English speaking branches.

### **IV. "Boring From Within" (The Second Period Tactics)**
*   **The Philosophy:** Lenin’s *Left-Wing Communism: An Infantile Disorder* ordered Communists to stop isolating themselves and enter mainstream unions to "destroy them from within."
    *   *Lenin’s Order:* "Resort to all sorts of devices, maneuvers, and illegal methods, to evasion and subterfuge, in order to penetrate into the trade unions."
*   **Labor Operations (TUEL):**
    *   **William Z. Foster:** The key figure. A former Syndicalist/I.W.W. leader turned Communist. He founded the *Trade Union Educational League* (TUEL) to capture the AFL.
    *   **Methods:** Character assassination of non-communist leaders ("Labor Fakers"), forming secret "nuclei" inside unions, disrupting meetings.
    *   **Results:**
        *   **Mining:** Attempted to capture the United Mine Workers (UMW) by opposing John L. Lewis. Lewis expelled them.
        *   **Needle Trades:** Success. They captured the Fur Workers (Ben Gold) and heavily infiltrated the Garment Workers (ILGWU), leading to disastrous strikes (e.g., Passaic, NJ).
*   **Political Operations (The Farmer-Labor Fiasco):**
    *   **Objective:** To capture the "Third Party" movement of Robert La Follette (1924).
    *   **The St. Paul Convention (1924):** The Communists used the "Federated Farmer-Labor Party" (a front) to pack the convention. They planned to nominate La Follette.
    *   **The Moscow Betrayal:** At the last minute, Moscow decided La Follette was a "petit bourgeois" trap and ordered the American CP to oppose him.
    *   **The Suicide:** The American communists, forced to denounce the man they had just spent months courting, nominated their own ticket (Foster for President, Gitlow for VP).
    *   **The Vote:** They received a laughable **33,361 votes** nationwide. This proved they had zero mass appeal and could only function through intrigue, not open politics.

### **V. The Moscow Solar System (The Invention of "Fronts")**
*   **The Architects:** **Otto Kuusinen** (Comintern) and **Willi Muenzenberg** (German Communist propaganda genius).
*   **The Concept:** The "Solar System." The Communist Party is the sun; around it orbit "mass organizations" (planets) that appear independent but are controlled by the Party.
*   **Terminology:**
    *   **"Innocents' Clubs":** Muenzenberg's term for organizations designed to rope in gullible liberals ("artists, professors, ministers").
    *   **"Transmission Belts":** Stalin's term for organizations that transmit the Party's will to the masses.
*   **Muenzenberg’s Cynicism:** He expressed "pitiless and jeering contempt" for the intellectuals he manipulated. They were puppets to him.
*   **Key Early Fronts:**
    *   **Friends of the Soviet Union (FSU):** Originally "Friends of Soviet Russia" (famine relief 1921). Used to whitewash the regime.
    *   **International Labor Defense (ILD):** A branch of the Russian *MOPR* (Red Aid). Ostensibly a legal defense fund for all workers; actually a partisan fund for Communists. (Later headed by Vito Marcantonio).
    *   **Anti-Imperialist League:** Designed to exploit colonial grievances.
    *   **Student/Youth Groups:** Early attempts to regiment youth, precursors to the massive *American Youth Congress* of the 1930s.

### **VI. The Purging of the American Party (1929)**
*   **Leadership Crisis:** The American party was plagued by factionalism (Ruthenberg/Lovestone faction vs. Foster/Cannon faction).
    *   *Nature of the Fight:* It was a proxy war mimicking the Stalin vs. Trotsky vs. Bukharin fights in Russia. It had no basis in American reality.
*   **The 1929 Decapitation:**
    *   **Jay Lovestone & Ben Gitlow:** By 1929, they controlled 90% of the American party membership and the convention delegates. They were the "majority."
    *   **The Summons:** Stalin summoned the American leadership to Moscow to settle the dispute.
    *   **The Commission:** A commission headed by Vyacheslav Molotov and Stalin investigated the Americans.
    *   **The Verdict:** Stalin decided Lovestone was a "Right Deviator" (allied with Bukharin). He ordered the majority leaders to resign and hand power to the minority (Foster/Browder).
    *   **The Confrontation:** Lovestone and Gitlow refused to confess to crimes they didn't commit. Stalin exploded:
        *   *Stalin's Quote:* "True courage consists in being strong enough to master and overcome one's self and subordinate one's will to the will of the collective."
    *   **The Aftermath:** Stalin warned them there was "plenty of room in the graveyards" for those who defied him. They fled Russia. Back in the US, the party membership—conditioned to obedience—instantly turned on Lovestone and Gitlow, expelling them. This proved the American party was a "colonial agency" with no democratic will of its own.

### **VII. A Milquetoast Takes Command (Earl Browder)**
*   **The Selection:** Why Earl Browder?
    *   After purging the brilliant (Trotsky) and the popular (Bukharin), Stalin preferred mediocrities. He hated "intellectuals."
    *   **William Z. Foster:** He was the logical choice, but Stalin distrusted him as too ambitious and too "American" (radical background). He was given a titular role but no power.
*   **Earl Browder’s Profile:**
    *   **Background:** A bookkeeper from Kansas. Weak, colorless, uninspiring.
    *   **The "Bookkeeper" Mentality:** He was a perfect bureaucrat. He would never challenge Moscow; he would simply audit the books and follow instructions.
    *   **Rise to Power:** He was appointed "General Secretary" (Stalin's title) not by the American members, but by Stalin's personal fiat.
    *   **The "Monolith":** Under Browder, the era of factionalism ended. The party became "monolithic"—a single block of obedient servants, shifting lines instantly upon receipt of cables from the Kremlin. This paved the way for the "Red Decade" where the party could successfully pretend to be anything (Revolutionary, Democratic, Isolationist) without internal dissent.

# 2. The "Third Period" & The Depression (1929–1935)
### **I. The Red Decade Dawns (The "Third Period" Theory)**
*   **The Decree of 1929:** On May 6, 1929, Stalin declared: "I think the moment is not far off when a revolutionary crisis will develop in America."
    *   *The "Developed" Crisis:* Party literature immediately shifted from the future tense to the present tense. It became dogma that the crisis *had* developed.
    *   *The "Paper Revolution":* Despite having only ~8,000 to 12,000 members (with a 75% turnover rate), the party acted as if it were leading millions. They reported to Moscow that they were "capturing the streets" (which usually meant a few hundred people rioting in Union Square).
*   **The Enemy: "Social Fascism":**
    *   **The Theory:** The main obstacle to revolution was not the capitalist (who was collapsing) or the fascist (who was crude), but the "Social Fascist"—the Socialist, the Liberal, and the Reformist Labor Leader who deluded the workers with democratic illusions.
    *   **The List of Enemies:** William Green (AFL), Norman Thomas (Socialist), and John L. Lewis were branded "fascist agents" and "murderers."
*   **Targeting the New Deal:**
    *   From 1933–1935, the Communists were the fiercest enemies of FDR.
    *   **The Manifesto:** A February 1935 manifesto was titled: "AGAINST THE 'NEW DEAL' OF HUNGER, FASCISM AND WAR!"
    *   **The Rhetoric:** The *Daily Worker* (Nov 11, 1933) ran the headline: "Roosevelt and Mussolini—Blood Brothers." The NRA (National Recovery Administration) was described as "new chains for labor."
*   **Negro Segregation ("Self-Determination in the Black Belt"):**
    *   **The Order:** Moscow ordered the American party to demand a separate "Negro Republic" in the Southern U.S., based on the Soviet "nationalities" model.
    *   **The Cynicism:** American Negroes did not want segregation; they wanted integration. The Communists forced this slogan on them anyway.
    *   **Exploitation:** The Party created the *League of Struggle for Negro Rights* (headed by Langston Hughes). They exploited the Scottsboro Boys case for fundraising and propaganda, often disregarding the safety of the defendants for the sake of "class war" publicity.

### **II. Fascism Has The Right Of Way (The German Catastrophe)**
*   **The Strategy of Suicide:** The "Social Fascism" policy led directly to the victory of Adolf Hitler.
    *   **"After Hitler, Our Turn":** The Kremlin believed that a Nazi victory would be temporary, would destroy the "Social Democratic illusions" of the workers, and precipitate a Communist revolution.
    *   **Refusal to Unite:** In December 1932 and March 1933, the German Social Democrats pleaded with the Communists for a United Front against Hitler. The Communists refused and ridiculed the offer.
    *   **Active Collaboration:** In 1931, on Moscow's orders, German Communists voted *with* the Nazis in the Prussian Landtag (referendum) to bring down the Social Democratic government.
*   **The Aftermath in America:**
    *   **Denial:** Even after Hitler crushed the German Communist Party, the Comintern insisted the policy was correct. Browder argued that Hitler was merely "clearing the ground" for the proletarian revolution.
    *   **The Madison Square Garden Riot (Feb 1934):** American Socialists and trade unionists held a rally to protest the shelling of workers' homes in Vienna by the Dollfuss regime. The Communists, viewing this as a "Social Fascist" trick, invaded the Garden and physically broke up the meeting, leading to a massive riot. This was the high-water mark of the "Third Period" insanity in the U.S.
*   **Union Busting (The TUUL):**
    *   **Trade Union Unity League (TUUL):** Under William Z. Foster, the party abandoned "boring from within" and switched to "dual unionism"—creating tiny Red unions to destroy AFL unions.
    *   **The Record:** They formed the *National Miners Union* (opposing John L. Lewis) and the *National Textile Workers Union*. They led the Gastonia strike into a bloody defeat to create martyrs (Fred Beal).
    *   **Goal:** Explicitly stated by Profintern head Lozovsky: "We want to break up the reformist unions... destroy the trade union apparatus."

### **III. The Cult of Russia-Worship**
*   **The Psychology of Distance:** Russia-worship was a "religious experience" for American liberals, a psychopathic flight from the reality of the Depression.
    *   **The Paradox:** As the Depression hit America, "Intellectuals hesitated between joining the Catholic Church and joining the Communist Party—they yearned for a faith."
    *   **Vicarious Sacrifice:** American admirers "made their sacrifices comfortably by overeating at banquets." They were willing to starve—but only vicariously, through the Russian peasants.
*   **The Reality (1929–1933):**
    *   **The Famine:** A man-made famine in the Ukraine and North Caucasus killed 3 to 7 million people. It was a "planned famine" to punish the peasantry for resisting collectivization.
    *   **The Terror:** The "Liquidation of the Kulaks as a class" involved deporting millions to the Arctic. The "Valuta" terror involved the GPU torturing citizens to extract gold and foreign currency (even holding children hostage).
    *   **Internal Passports:** The regime instituted internal passports, tying workers to their factories—a return to feudal serfdom.
*   **The "Tourist" Phenomenon:**
    *   **The Pilgrims:** 85% of foreign tourists in Russia were Americans. They walked like "somnambulists," ignoring queues and starvation.
    *   **The Anecdote:** Lyons describes a dinner at Town Hall in NY for "Americans who had been to Russia." A gray-haired lady praised the "moral beauty" of the GPU; a clergyman rejoiced in the "Liquidation of God."
    *   **The Painter:** A NY painter sketched the truth in his notebook (beggars, hunger) but spoke only lies to his friends. He couldn't bear to shatter the illusion.

### **IV. The Liberals Invent a Utopia**
*   **The "Plan" Fetish:** Liberals were seduced by the *concept* of the Five-Year Plan. They contrasted "Capitalist Chaos" with "Soviet Order."
    *   *Stuart Chase:* "Why should the Russians have all the fun of remaking a world?" (Written while millions were dying).
*   **The Liberal Press:** *The Nation* and *The New Republic* served as the primary apologists.
    *   **The "Negative Pregnant":** When Hearst papers published sensationalized famine stories, the liberal press exposed Hearst's errors to imply *no* famine existed.
    *   **Rationalizing Terror:** The *New Republic* defended the execution of 48 professors and engineers by asking, "Are official rifle squads of the Cheka any more depressing... than the hired Cossacks of American mill towns?"
*   **Specific Examples of Hallucination:**
    *   **Eduard C. Lindeman (New Republic, 1933):** Visited during the famine. Reported "There is stability... solidarity." He saw a crowd helping a traffic cop and called it "an illustration of voluntary collectivism."
    *   **Congressman Henry T. Rainey:** Visited during the "passportization" (serfdom) decrees. Reported: "Labor is freer in Russia than in any other country."
    *   **Sherwood Eddy:** Stated "there is very little convict labor in Russia" at the exact moment the GPU was building the White Sea Canal with 250,000 slave laborers.
    *   **Julian Huxley & John Dewey:** Praised Soviet science and education as "liberating" at the moment the curriculum was being purged of all non-Marxist thought and "pedology" (child study) was outlawed.

### **V. Apologists Do Their Stuff (The Press Agents)**
Lyons dissects the key figures who mediated the Soviet myth to the West:
1.  **Walter Duranty (*NY Times*):** The "Dean" of apologists.
    *   *The Philosophy:* "You can't make an omelet without breaking eggs." He admitted the brutality but justified it as "Asiatic" and successful.
    *   *The Famine:* He famously wrote: "There is no actual starvation or deaths from starvation but there is widespread mortality from diseases due to malnutrition."
    *   *Motive:* Intellectual arrogance. He viewed the Russian masses as cattle who needed a whip.
2.  **Louis Fischer (*The Nation*):** The "Insider."
    *   *The Method:* He wrote with the authority of someone consulted by the Commissars. He attacked anyone who criticized the terror.
    *   *The GPU:* He described the GPU (secret police) as a "vast industrial organization and a big educational institution" that administered a "cure through labor."
    *   *The Silence:* When he finally broke with Stalin in 1941 (*Men and Politics*), he admitted he had known about the famine and terror all along but kept silent to "save the revolution."
3.  **Maurice Hindus:** The "Sentimentalist."
    *   *The Method:* He diluted the horror. In *The Great Offensive* (1933), he described the famine area's "distress" but blamed it on "mismanagement" rather than government policy. He "set the table for the dead son" to keep the American liberals happy.
4.  **Sidney & Beatrice Webb:** The "Scholars."
    *   *The Book:* *Soviet Communism: A New Civilization?* (1,200 pages).
    *   *Method:* They described the administrative *paperwork* as reality. They proved the Supreme Soviet was a parliament because the law said so. They asserted "Stalin is not a dictator" because he was merely the Secretary of the Party.
5.  **The "Dean of Canterbury" (Hewlett Johnson):**
    *   Wrote *The Soviet Power*. Described Russia as "truly Christian in spirit" and the "absence of fear."

### **VI. The Red Cultural Renaissance ("Proletcult")**
*   **The Goal:** To capture the American mind by redefining art.
    *   *The Slogan:* "Art is a weapon in the class struggle."
*   **Proletcult (Proletarian Culture):**
    *   **The Formula:** Literature must be about the "workers." It must be optimistic ("Up" books, not "Down" books). It must follow the "dialectic."
    *   **RAPP (Russian Association of Proletarian Writers):** The Soviet body that dictated literary trends. American writers (Michael Gold, Joseph Freeman) attended the Kharkov Congress (1930) and returned with orders to "Bolshevize" American art.
*   **The Institutions:**
    *   **New Masses:** The "cultural" organ. Used to scourge heretics.
    *   **John Reed Clubs:** Network of clubs for young writers. Incubators for the party line. Published magazines like *Left Front*, *Blast*, *Anvil*.
    *   **The Theater:** *New Theater League*, *Theater Union*, *Group Theater*. Produced "agit-prop" (agitation-propaganda) plays like *Waiting for Lefty*.
*   **The Terror:**
    *   Writers were bullied into conformity. **Thornton Wilder** was attacked for "petty bourgeois mentality." **Granville Hicks** publicly apologized for liking Proust.
    *   **The Standard:** "A poet becomes more of a poet by writing about economic problems."
*   **The League of American Writers (1935):**
    *   Founded at the First American Writers Congress.
    *   **Founding Fathers:** Earl Browder, Michael Gold, Granville Hicks, Theodore Dreiser, Waldo Frank, Langston Hughes.
    *   **The Pledge:** Members implicitly agreed to the "People's Front" program. It was a mechanism to corral famous names (Hemingway, Lewis) behind Communist resolutions.

### **VII. More Planets Are Launched (Building the Solar System)**
*   **The "Solar System" Strategy:** Building a ring of "mass organizations" around the Party to serve as "transmission belts."
*   **The "League Against War and Fascism" (The Masterpiece):**
    *   **Origin:** The Amsterdam Congress (1932), run by Henri Barbusse and Willi Muenzenberg.
    *   **U.S. Launch:** A "U.S. Congress Against War" (1933). Dr. J.B. Matthews (a fellow traveler then) was Chairman. Donald Henderson (Communist) was Secretary.
    *   **The Deception:** It enlisted pacifists, church groups, and students under the banner of "Peace," but its platform was "Defense of the Soviet Union."
    *   **The Soldier Incident:** At the first congress, a U.S. soldier appeared on stage and pledged to "sabotage the army" if it fought Russia. Browder boasted of this treason to the Comintern.
    *   **Dr. Harry F. Ward:** Head of the League. A professor of Christian Ethics who served as a loyal instrument for an atheist dictatorship.
*   **The Capture of Youth:**
    *   **National Student League (NSL):** The open Communist group.
    *   **American Youth Congress (AYC):** Founded by Viola Ilma as a broad youth federation. The Communists "packed" the first convention (1934), ousted Ilma, and installed a "Presidium" controlled by the Young Communist League (Gil Green, Joseph Lash).
    *   **Eleanor Roosevelt:** She was drawn into the AYC, invited leaders to the White House, and defended them against "Red-baiting," providing the ultimate respectability for a Stalinist front.
*   **Other Key Planets:**
    *   **Friends of the Soviet Union (FSU):** Headed by Corliss Lamont.
    *   **International Labor Defense (ILD):** Headed by Vito Marcantonio.
    *   **National Negro Congress:** Captured to replace the ineffective *League of Struggle for Negro Rights*.
    *   **Workers Alliance:** Organization of the unemployed (WPA workers), controlled by Herbert Benjamin (Communist).

# 3. The Popular Front & The "Trojan Horse" (1935–1939)
### **I. Moscow Adopts The Trojan Horse (The Strategic Pivot)**
*   **The Catalyst:** The rise of Nazi Germany was the *sole* reason for the change. Stalin realized the "Third Period" policy (Social Fascism), which split the German working class, had handed power to Hitler. He now desperately needed military alliances with the "Pluto-Democracies" (France, England, USA).
*   **The 7th World Congress (Moscow, Summer 1935):**
    *   **Georgi Dimitrov's Speech:** The Comintern Secretary General mocked the "cut-and-dried" sectarianism of the past. He explicitly used the metaphor of the **Trojan Horse**: "We must penetrate the very heart of the enemy" by hiding inside "mass organizations."
    *   **The New Order:** Communists must stop attacking the bourgeoisie and instead pose as the defenders of "bourgeois democracy" against Fascism.
    *   **The Rationalization:** The "Revolution" was not abandoned, merely "postponed." This was a "tactic."
*   **The Russian Context (Nationalism Revived):**
    *   Domestically, Stalin revived "Great Russian" nationalism. The "International" anthem was replaced by patriotic songs. Heroes like Peter the Great and Prince Vladimir were rehabilitated.
    *   **The American Mirror:** The American Communist Party mirrored this by suddenly worshipping Lincoln, Jefferson, and Paine.
    *   **The Paradox:** As Russia became *more* totalitarian (the Great Terror began in 1935, erasing the Old Bolsheviks), its foreign agents shouted louder about "Democracy" and "Civil Liberties."

### **II. Communism Becomes Americanism (The Great Masquerade)**
*   **The Slogan:** Earl Browder coined the phrase: **"Communism is Twentieth-Century Americanism."**
    *   *The Visuals:* At the 9th Convention, the Red Flag was relegated to the corner; the Stars and Stripes took center stage. Portraits of Marx/Lenin were flanked by Washington/Lincoln.
    *   *The "Descendants":* Browder claimed the CPUSA was the "political descendant of the traditions of 1776 and 1861" and asserted they were the true "Sons and Daughters of the American Revolution."
*   **The New Deal Reconciliation:**
    *   Overnight, FDR changed from a "Fascist" and "Wall Street Tool" to a "Progressive Leader" essential for stopping Hitler.
    *   **Political Coalition:** The Party worked through the *American Labor Party* (NY), *Washington Commonwealth Federation*, and *Labor's Non-Partisan League* to turn out votes for FDR in 1936.
*   **The "Social Lobby" in Washington:**
    *   **Infiltration:** Washington was flooded with communist functionaries. The "shortest route to a government job was through East 13th Street" (Party HQ).
    *   **Bureaucratic Colonization:** The new agencies (WPA, NLRB, La Follette Committee) were staffed with "polysyllabic sophisticates" who talked Veblen and Marx.
    *   **The Scene:** Lyons describes a dizzying social life of cocktail parties where New Deal bureaucrats mingled with Party organizers.
*   **The "Penthouse Bolsheviks":**
    *   **The Phenomenon:** Wealthy Americans, suffering from Depression-guilt, flocked to the cause. It became "smart" to be Red.
    *   **The Anecdote:** Lyons describes a party in a swanky East River mansion where "liveried butlers" served champagne to guests singing the *Internationale* ("Arise ye prisoners of starvation").
    *   **Motivation:** It offered the "thrill of conspiracy" with the safety of conformity. One could be a revolutionary and a social climber simultaneously.

### **III. The American League for Soviet War Mongering**
*   **The Rebranding:** The *League Against War and Fascism* (founded 1933) was the largest front. In 1937 (Pittsburgh Congress), it was sanitized and renamed the **American League for Peace and Democracy**.
    *   **Membership:** Claimed 7.5 million affiliates (mostly through unsuspecting trade unions, church groups, and YWCAs).
    *   **Leadership:** Headed by **Dr. Harry F. Ward** (Union Theological Seminary), a "Red Dean" who provided clerical cover for atheist maneuvers. The real power lay with **Browder** (Vice-President) and the Party caucus.
