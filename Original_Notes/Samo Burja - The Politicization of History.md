### **1. Technological Backsliding & The Bronze Age Collapse**
*   **The Ratchet Fallacy:**
    *   The modern Whig view of history assumes knowledge is a "one-way ratchet"—once a technology (like writing, plumbing, or metallurgy) is invented, it cannot be lost.
    *   Burja argues this is demonstrably false. Civilizations frequently slide backward, losing technologies that their ancestors possessed due to institutional collapse or secrecy.
*   **Case Study: Greek Fire (The Lost Weapon):**
    *   *The Tech:* A Byzantine incendiary weapon (essentially Napalm) used on ships and even in **hand-held flamethrowers**.
    *   *The Anachronism:* It destroys our intuition of "linear progress" to imagine a 7th-century soldier wielding a flamethrower.
    *   *The Loss:* It was a state secret so well-kept that when the Byzantine bureaucracy dissolved, the formula was lost. The "advanced" successors (Ottomans/Europeans) could not replicate it for centuries.
*   **Case Study: The Late Bronze Age Collapse (~1200 BC):**
    *   *The Scale:* A civilization-wide systems collapse that destroyed the Hittite Empire, Mycenaean Greece, and the Near East. Only Egypt barely survived (though it ceased monumental construction for a century).
    *   *The "Unsent Email":* Burja cites a specific clay tablet found in a ruined city: a desperate plea from a Hittite prince asking for military reinforcements against an unknown enemy.
        *   *The Detail:* Archaeologists believe the tablet was never sent; it was **baked by the fire that burned the city down**. It is a frozen moment of apocalyptic panic.
    *   *Total Amnesia:*
        *   **Literacy:** The Greeks stopped writing for 400 years. Linear B (Mycenaean script) vanished completely. When literacy returned, they had to borrow a totally new alphabet from the Phoenicians.
        *   **Architecture:** The later Greeks looked at the ruins of Mycenaean palaces (huge stone walls) and invented myths about **Cyclops and Giants** building them. They psychologically could not conceive that their own ancestors had the capacity to move such stones.
        *   **Geopolitics:** The Hittite Empire, a superpower peer to Egypt (Pharaohs called Hittite Kings "Brother"), was so thoroughly destroyed that it was forgotten until archaeologists rediscovered it in the 19th century.
    *   *The Fragility of Trade:* The Bronze Age was highly globalized (tin from Afghanistan/Britain mixed with copper from Cyprus). When the security environment collapsed, trade stopped, bronze production halted, and the complex society starved. **Decentralization is not insurance against collapse.**

    *   *Controversial Claim:* **The Spanish Empire was a net moral improvement over the Aztec Empire.**
    *   *The Alliance:* Cortez didn't defeat the Aztecs with 500 Spaniards. He defeated them with 500 Spaniards and thousands of Tlaxcalans (local natives).

    *   *The "Giants" Myth:* When Native Americans told settlers that "Giants lived here before us," they were likely describing a previous, collapsed civilization whose ruins they inhabited but did not build (just as Greeks viewed Mycenaean ruins).
